WeeTrip

Across Southeast Asia, there are many communities that are still in poverty. As we come from positions of privilege, it is only fitting that we extend helping hands to our neighbours who are in need.

WeeTrip is an annual overseas service project organised by WeeVolunteer for students in WKWSCI. Over the years, teams have various countries in Southeast Asia, including Laos and Vietnam.

We aim to offer different yet sustainable volunteer opportunities with a communication focus, by embarking on long-term projects that can make use of our skills as communications students.

Past Projects

2018 2017 2016
Luang Prabang City and
Ban Houehin Village, Oudomxay Province, Laos
8 - 20 December 2018

The team spent a week in Ban Houehin Village building a relationship with the welcoming villagers and assisted with assembling a water filtration system for the village. They then spent another five days in the city centre making use of their communication skills by collecting stories and capturing images to spread awareness about lesser-known stories and issues facing our neighbours in Laos.

Trip Highlights

Promoting sustainable tourism

As communication students, we wanted to use what we have learnt in school to benefit communities with a wider impact.

Water filtration system

Laos is a landlocked country and potable water is scarce. The water filtration system can help with the problem of clean water scarcity.

English lessons and learning aids

All students in Laos have to learn English from primary school, but students rarely get to use the language in their daily lives.
